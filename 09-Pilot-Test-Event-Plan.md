# Section 9 (Pivot): Pilot Test Event — Rented Venue, No-Combat, 18+, Single Weekend

**STRATEGIC PIVOT (Jul 2026):** the founders have decided to de-risk the full land-acquisition venture (`02`–`08`) by first running a **standalone pilot event at a rented venue/farm** — a single 3-day weekend, **no combat, 18+ only, no overnight stay**, in **late fall**, built around an **app-based in-person quest/game** coordinating vendors and activities for **200–1,000 attendees**. This is explicitly a learning/validation exercise before committing the capital modeled in `07-Startup-Costs-and-Capital-Plan.md` and `08-Financial-Projections.md` — those documents remain valid for the eventual owned-land venture, but are **not** the near-term plan. `09-Risk-Register.md` and `10-Roadmap-and-Timeline.md` are deferred until after this pilot informs them.

---

## 9.1 Why This Pivot Is Sound (Grounded in Prior Research)

- **No land purchase/lease-to-own commitment, no site development capex** (`07-Startup-Costs-and-Capital-Plan.md` §8.2–8.3 entirely avoided for this pilot) — the single largest risk/capital item in the full plan is removed.
- **No combat, no overnight** removes the weapons-safety framework (`03-Regulatory-Legal-and-Insurance.md` §4.3, deferred), the Kentucky minor-waiver exposure (moot at 18+), and all lodging/glamping capex (`07` §8.4) in one move.
- **A rented venue shifts insurance/liability to a much more standard, well-understood category** — single-day/weekend general liability event insurance (already identified as the likely near-term product via K&K Insurance, `03-Regulatory-Legal-and-Insurance.md` §4.6) is a mature, easily-quoted product for a rented-venue day event, more so than for a multi-day owned campground.
- **This directly tests the hardest operational unknowns** (staffing at 200–1,000 scale, vendor coordination, an app-driven quest experience, crowd flow) **without the confounding variable of also being a new landowner** — a genuinely good de-risking sequence.

## 9.3 Venue — Real Rental Cost Data

Sourced from farm/barn event-venue pricing (wedding-industry data, the closest real comparable for "rent an existing venue/farm for a multi-day event"):

| Venue scale | Capacity | Typical rental cost |
|---|---|---|
| Mid-size barn/farm venue | 120–250 guests | $2,000–$6,000/day, $4,000–$10,000/weekend (2–3 day) |
| Larger farm/event venue | up to ~300–450 guests | $4,500–$10,000/weekend |

**Honest gap: none of the sourced venue data covers the 500–1,000 capacity end of the stated target range** — that scale moves into fairgrounds, festival grounds, or large event-lawn territory (a different rental category than a wedding barn), which was **not directly sourced in this research pass**. **Action item:** once the founders settle on a target attendance within 200–1,000, get **direct rental quotes from 3–5 candidate venues** in the target region — a 250-person event and a 1,000-person event are functionally different venue searches, not the same search at different price points.

**Recommendation:** given this is explicitly a **test/validation event**, target the **lower half of the stated range (200–400)** for the first pilot — this keeps venue cost in the well-sourced $2,000–$10,000/weekend band, keeps vendor/staffing coordination complexity more manageable for a first attempt, and still generates genuinely useful data on crowd flow, app-based quest engagement, and vendor coordination before scaling to 500–1,000 in a second pilot or the eventual owned venue.

## 9.4 App-Based Quest/Game Platform — Real Options

Real, currently-operating platforms built specifically for this use case (venue-wide, app/QR-based, multiplayer, live leaderboard):

| Platform | Model | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **ReadySet** (playreadysetgo.com) | Organizer studio + player app; custom venue map, missions (photo, trivia, head-to-head, AR) | Built explicitly for "turn any venue into a live multiplayer experience" — closest direct fit to the stated concept; positioned for brands/agencies/event teams, so pricing is likely custom-quoted |
| **Proxi** (proxi.co) | No-download, browser/link-based scavenger hunt builder | Real-time organizer dashboard, live map, QR/link join — lower-friction (no app install) than a dedicated app |
| **PlayTours** (playtours.app) | No-install, browser-based; supports team play, QR/location check-in, chat | Explicitly cites running "1000 pax games with only 4 facilitators" — directly relevant to our upper attendance target; has a free trial tier |
| **Gonow Play** | AI-verified photo-scavenger-hunt, progressive web app (no app store) | Built for events/parties/exhibitions; AI auto-verifies photo submissions, reducing facilitator labor |
| **Questovery** | GPS/QR-triggered step-by-step quest routes, organizer web dashboard + player app | Offers a pay-per-participating-team tier for independent organizers — likely the most accessible pricing model for a first pilot |

**Recommendation:** for a first pilot at 200–400 attendees, prioritize a **no-download, browser/QR-based platform (Proxi, PlayTours, or Gonow Play)** over a dedicated native app — this removes app-store friction for a one-time event audience who won't install an app for a single weekend, and several of these platforms have low-commitment/pay-per-use pricing tiers appropriate for a test event budget. **Get direct demo/pricing quotes from at least 2–3 of these before committing** — none of the pricing is fully public, and it should be benchmarked against actual attendance target and feature needs (AI photo verification vs. simple QR check-ins vs. AR).

## 9.6 Promotion and Marketing — Influencer/Creator Partnership Research

This was flagged by the founders as the chief current challenge. Real findings:

### A. Realistic-scale local/regional creators (not celebrity-tier)

For a 200–1,000 person regional test event, **nano/micro-influencers (1K–50K followers) in the target region and niche are the right tier to approach** — not the massive brand-campaign examples in part C below, which require budgets and production scale far beyond a pilot event.

- **Spacecowboy_af (Louisville, KY)** — lifestyle/food/festival content creator, 39.3K Instagram followers, named "Best Local Influencer" in LEO Weekly's 2025 Readers' Choice Awards. Publicly covers local festivals (e.g., Bourbon & Beyond) with a stated process of researching an event in advance and covering it from multiple angles (fashion, food, atmosphere) — **a strong, concrete, real example of exactly the kind of local creator partnership to pursue** for a Louisville-adjacent event.
- **Nashville-based creators** (e.g., Jules P/Julessocialstorytelling — local events/restaurant/owner-interview content; Lauren Dauk — lifestyle/travel content with hospitality-brand partnership experience) — Nashville has a visibly active local UGC/lifestyle creator market that regularly covers local events and venue partnerships.
- **Niche fit beyond general "local lifestyle":** the Renaissance faire/cosplay/LARP-adjacent audience is real and active on TikTok/Pinterest (confirmed via real search results showing dedicated "Renaissance Fair influencer" and "medieval cosplay" content categories) — this is a **more precisely-targeted niche audience** than generic local lifestyle influencers, and worth a separate, parallel outreach track from the general local-event creators above.

### B. Influencer discovery/marketplace tools (for scaling outreach beyond manual search)

| Platform | What it does | Pricing signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Lessie AI** | Natural-language search across 50M+ creator profiles (TikTok/IG/YouTube/LinkedIn/Twitch) by city/niche, with verified contact emails | $19.90/month, 1,000 search credits |
| **MicroMatching** | Marketplace connecting brands with 1K–50K-follower micro-influencers; has an explicit "sell out your next event" synchronized-posting product | Free for brands during pilot phase; 15% commission on influencer payouts |
| **BrandPiko** | Geo/niche-filtered micro-influencer discovery (1K–50K followers) across IG/YouTube/TikTok, cost-first sorting by CPM/CPC | Plans from $19–$99/month |
| **Influship** | Natural-language micro-influencer search combining niche, location, audience quality | Not publicly priced — contact for quote |
| **Mustard** | Local influencer campaign packages by city, bundles multiple local creators into one campaign | Promotional package examples cited: ~18 local influencers/18 posts for a bundled campaign fee |

**Recommendation:** given the founders' stated budget constraints (per `07`/`08`), a **low-cost discovery tool (Lessie AI or BrandPiko, ~$20–$100/month) combined with direct manual outreach to specific identified local creators (like Spacecowboy_af and comparable Nashville/Lexington creators)** is more appropriate for a pilot-stage budget than a managed campaign platform like Mustard, which bundles cost into a package fee.

### C. Strategic pattern worth borrowing (at appropriate scale) from large-brand case studies

Real, sourced examples (MrBeast x Shopify/Feastables NYC heist activation, Salesforce x MrBeast Super Bowl scavenger hunt, BTS x CookieRun #RiddleKingdom global hunt, Niantic/Paramount's "Running Man" city-wide quest) all share one structural insight directly applicable at any budget scale: **the game itself becomes the shareable content — attendees filming their own quest progress and posting it organically is the actual distribution mechanism**, not a separate marketing campaign layered on top. **Practical takeaway for our pilot:** design the app-based quest with built-in "shareable moments" (a photo-mission checkpoint, a branded AR moment, a leaderboard screenshot worth posting) — several of the platforms in §9.4 (Gonow Play, ReadySet) already support photo/AR missions natively — so that **attendees' own social posts become a meaningful secondary promotion channel**, supplementing the direct influencer outreach in §9.6A–B rather than relying on influencer partnerships alone.

### D. Concrete next-step action plan

1. **Finalize target attendance (within 200–400 for a first pilot, per §9.3) and exact venue region** — outreach can't be targeted until the event's actual geography is set.
2. **Directly contact 5–10 identified local/regional creators** in the target region and adjacent Renaissance-faire/cosplay niche (using Spacecowboy_af and comparable Nashville creators as concrete starting examples, plus a targeted search for equivalent creators in the actual host city once chosen).
3. **Set up a low-cost discovery tool trial** (Lessie AI or BrandPiko) to build a broader outreach list beyond the handful of creators identified by name here.
4. **Design at least one "shareable moment" directly into the app-based quest** so organic attendee content supplements paid/partnership promotion.
5. **Budget a modest influencer/creator line item** (not yet sized — needs a real quote once specific creators are contacted; the platforms in §9.6B suggest a $500–$2,500 range is realistic for a handful of micro-influencer partnerships at this event scale, based on the "$500 → $2,500" framing cited by BrandPiko and the bundled local-campaign pricing cited by Mustard, **though neither is a direct quote for our specific event**).

## 9.7 Key Takeaways

1. **The pivot to a rented-venue pilot is a sound de-risking move** — it removes the two largest capital/risk items (land acquisition, combat safety) from the full plan while directly testing the hardest unproven operational question: coordinating 200–1,000 people with vendors and an app-based quest.
2. **Target the lower half of the stated attendance range (200–400) for this first pilot** — venue cost data is well-sourced at that scale ($2,000–$10,000/weekend); the 500–1,000 end of the range needs its own separate venue research pass once a first pilot validates the operating model.
3. **A no-download, browser/QR-based quest platform (Proxi, PlayTours, or Gonow Play) is the recommended starting point** over a dedicated native app, given the one-time-event audience and lower-friction install requirement.
4. **Vendor coordination should be woven directly into the quest mechanic** (checkpoints at vendor stalls) — this both drives vendor foot traffic and is the strongest pitch to attract vendors to an unproven first event.
5. **Promotion should combine three tracks**: (a) direct outreach to named, real local/regional micro-influencers (Spacecowboy_af and comparable creators), (b) a low-cost influencer-discovery tool to scale that outreach, and (c) designing the quest itself to generate organic attendee-shared content, per the large-brand case-study pattern.
6. **This pilot's results should directly inform a rebuild of `07-Startup-Costs-and-Capital-Plan.md` and `08-Financial-Projections.md`** before committing to the full land-based venture — real staffing, vendor, and attendance-conversion data from this event will replace several of the modeled assumptions in those documents.
