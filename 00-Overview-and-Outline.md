## 1.1 Business Description

The Company will acquire and develop a single, multi-purpose plot of land into a permanent, reusable venue for immersive live-action role-play (LARP) and themed live-event experiences. Unlike a single-genre LARP campground, the property will be built as flexible "set" infrastructure — modular structures, terrain features, and staging areas that can be re-skinned to support multiple recurring event brands under one company, e.g.:

- **Halfling Hollow** — fantasy/pastoral village theme
- **Colonial Village** — historical/frontier settlement theme
- **Wastelands** (Mad Max / Half-Life-style) — post-apocalyptic theme
- Additional themed events added over time as the venue matures

The Company is the **landowner and venue operator**, not necessarily the sole event producer. Revenue comes from a mix of:

1. **Direct event production** — the Company runs its own branded events (ticket sales, tiered accommodations, add-on services).
2. **Venue rental / hosting** — third-party LARP organizations, reenactment groups, corporate team-building groups, or film/photo productions rent the land and infrastructure.
3. **Ancillary services** — lodging tiers (bring-your-own camping up to fully furnished themed tents/cabins), food and beverage, in-character vendor stalls, photography/videography packages, equipment/costume rental, and parking/security services bundled into ticket tiers.

Attendees pay for **tiered access**, from a base "bring your own gear, camp yourself" pass to a premium "fully outfitted, themed, catered" pass. In-character actors/NPCs (paid staff, contracted actors, or volunteer community members) staff shops, quest-lines, and encounters. The Company also offers **in-house photo/video capture** as a paid add-on so guests can stay phone-free and in-character during the event ("digital detox" as a product feature, not just a value statement).

This is fundamentally a **hybrid of four existing, provable business models**, which we will use as financial and operational reference points throughout this plan (see `02-Market-and-Comparables.md`):

- **Renaissance faires / historical reenactment campgrounds** (e.g., Texas Renaissance Festival, Bristol Renaissance Faire, Pennsic War / Society for Creative Anachronism war-camps)
- **Commercial LARP organizations** (e.g., Dystopia Rising, Mixed Nuts Entertainment's Wayfinder Experience, Knight Realms, Amtgard chapters)
- **Glamping / outdoor experiential venues and campgrounds** (KOA franchise economics, glamping resort cost data)
- **Immersive/experiential entertainment companies** (Meow Wolf, escape rooms, murder-mystery weekend venues)

We will not invent numbers for any of these — where hard data exists publicly, we will cite it; where it does not (most LARP orgs are small private companies or nonprofits that don't disclose financials), we will say so explicitly and mark the figure as an assumption requiring validation.

## 1.2 Mission Statement

> **The Company exists to give people a real, physical place to disconnect from screens and reconnect with each other — building unforgettable, story-driven experiences in the real world that create genuine human connection, community, and memory.**

Supporting principles:

- **Real world over digital world.** Success is measured partly by how little guests need their phones during an event, not by social media impressions.
- **Connection over consumption.** The product is shared experience and community, not a transaction — repeat attendance and community formation are core KPIs, not just single-event ticket sales.
- **Unforgettable over convenient.** We accept higher operational complexity (costumed staff, built sets, live game design) in exchange for a product that a generic campground or festival cannot replicate.

This mission statement is a design constraint, not just marketing copy — it should be checked against every operational decision in this plan (e.g., Wi-Fi policy, phone check-in stations, photography service business case).

## 1.3 Honesty Checkpoint (per your conversational rules)

Before going further, some blunt framing so we plan with eyes open:

- **This is a capital-intensive, seasonal, weather-exposed, niche-market business.** Comparable businesses (Renaissance faires, SCA war-camps, commercial LARP campgrounds like Cold Hollow Land in NY used by Dystopia Rising, or Fort Meigs-style rented historical sites) generally take **years** to reach profitability, and several well-known LARP campgrounds/venues have historically operated at break-even or relied on volunteer labor to survive.
- **The addressable market is small relative to general outdoor recreation.** U.S. LARP participation is a few hundred thousand active hobbyists nationally at most (no authoritative census exists — this is a known data gap we'll flag, not fabricate a number for).
- **Land development for assembly/event use is a regulatory-heavy path** (zoning, mass gathering permits, septic/well capacity, fire marshal sign-off, ADA) — this is not "buy land, put up tents."
- **Insurance is a real and non-trivial cost line**, especially with simulated combat (boffer weapons, foam weapons, live steel reenactment). Specialty insurers exist for this (e.g., K&K Insurance has historically written reenactment/LARP event policies) but coverage terms, exclusions, and premiums must be obtained directly — we will not guess a specific premium number without either a quote or a clearly labeled rough industry range.
- We will build this plan to survive investor/lender scrutiny, which means every assumption gets a source or a "TO VALIDATE" flag.

## 1.5 Information I Need From You to Proceed Accurately

To keep every later number grounded rather than invented, I need your input (can be rough/best-guess) on:

1. **Geography** — target U.S. region/state (land cost, zoning law, and climate/season length vary enormously by state).
2. **Founding team** — how many of the 3–5 people are full-time vs. part-time, and what capital each can personally contribute (affects org structure and financing mix).
3. **Capital appetite** — are we planning a bank loan (needs collateral, usually land + conservative projections), equity investors (need higher growth story), or a blend?
4. **Scale ambition for Year 1** — are we thinking a first event of ~50 attendees, ~200, ~500+? This drastically changes land size, staffing, and capex needed for a realistic Phase 1.
5. **Existing assets** — does anyone already have land, LARP organizing experience, reenactment/costuming inventory, or an existing player community to seed attendance?
