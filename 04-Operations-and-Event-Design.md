## 5.1 Event Cadence — Year 1 and Beyond

Per the founders' stated target (3–4 events in Year 1, scaling from 30–50 to ~200 attendees) compared against comparables:

- **Knight Realms** runs *monthly* — but only after 28 years of infrastructure and community-building. **Dystopia Rising** chapters typically run **every 4–8 weeks**. **Pennsic** and **TRF** are single-annual (or multi-weekend seasonal) large-scale events.
- **A quarterly cadence (3–4 events/year) is a realistic and conservative Year 1 starting point** — it matches the founders' stated target, avoids overloading a small volunteer/founder team, and leaves recovery/rebuild time between events for site maintenance (a real cost, see `07-Startup-Costs-and-Capital-Plan.md`, pending).
- **Recommended Year 1 structure**, sequenced to de-risk the first outing and build toward the 200-person target:

| Event # | Theme | Target attendance | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Single theme (recommend **Halfling Hollow** — the most naturally suited to a cozy, low-intensity, theater-of-mind narrative style, easiest to run safely first) | 30–50 | Prove operational basics: check-in, waivers, sanitation, general safety, NPC scheduling |
| 2 | Same theme, repeat | ~75–100 | Test repeat-attendance/retention, refine based on Event 1 feedback |
| 3 | Second theme (e.g., **Colonial Village**) | ~100–150 | Prove multi-theme capability, test whether guests attend across themes |
| 4 | Either theme, or first outside-group venue rental (per the Pennsic/Cooper's Lake hosting model in `01-Market-and-Comparables.md` §2.1D) | ~150–200 | Push toward Year 1-end target; test the rental-revenue vertical in parallel |

**Rationale for starting with Halfling Hollow:** with combat deferred to Phase 3+ (`03-Regulatory-Legal-and-Insurance.md`), the sequencing logic is no longer about injury-risk ranking across themes — **all Year 1–2 themes, including Wastelands, run as non-combat/theater-of-mind experiences** and carry a comparable baseline risk profile (general outdoor/recreational hazards only, per `03-Regulatory-Legal-and-Insurance.md` §4.6). Halfling Hollow is still the recommended first event because its cozy, lower-intensity narrative tone is the easiest to execute well with a first-time volunteer/NPC cast, not because of a safety differential — **Wastelands can be sequenced earlier than previously planned if the founders prefer**, since its higher perceived intensity is now a narrative/tone consideration rather than a liability one.

## 5.2 Staffing Model

### NPC / volunteer labor (the industry-standard cost control)

As established in `01-Market-and-Comparables.md`, **Dystopia Rising's model of requiring paid attendees to work a 4–6 hour "NPC" (antagonist/extra) shift in exchange for their ticket** is the dominant mechanism the LARP industry uses to staff monster/antagonist/background roles without paying a full cast. **Knight Realms goes further and makes NPC participation entirely free** (no ticket cost at all for pure NPC attendees). **Recommendation:** adopt a hybrid — offer a **discounted "NPC/volunteer" ticket tier** (reduced price in exchange for a shift) similar to Dystopia Rising's model, which both controls labor cost and is a proven, guest-accepted convention in this hobby (i.e., it won't feel like an imposition to LARP-experienced attendees).

### Paid staff and security — real ratio data

There is **no single universal legal ratio** for event security staffing (confirmed by multiple industry sources — UK's "Purple Guide," California BSIS guidance, and private security firms all converge on this same point) — required staffing is risk-based, not attendance-based alone. That said, published **indicative ratios** exist and are directly useful for budgeting:

| Context | Indicative ratio | Source basis |
|---|---|---|
| Low-risk outdoor event, controlled access, minimal/no alcohol | 1 steward/security per 100–250 attendees | Purple Guide (Event Industry Forum) |
| Higher-risk (alcohol service, multi-zone, evening programming) | 1 per 75–100 attendees | Purple Guide; California BSIS guidance |
| General festival benchmark (broad U.S. commercial guidance) | 1 per 75–100 attendees | Multiple U.S. event-security firms |

**Applying this to our scale, given the non-combat Years 1–2 model:** at 30–50 attendees, ratio math alone would suggest fewer than 1 security staff, and with combat deferred (`03-Regulatory-Legal-and-Insurance.md` §4.6), that low-end estimate is now closer to appropriate than it would be in a combat scenario. **A practical floor of 1–2 general safety/stewarding staff per event is a reasonable Year 1–2 minimum** — enough to cover check-in security, basic crowd stewarding, and a redundant point of contact (avoiding a single point of failure), without the combat-driven "immediate dedicated responder" requirement that would apply once physical contact combat is introduced. At ~200 attendees, plan for **2–3 general safety/stewarding staff** using the low end of the low-risk ratio band. **No weapons-check station staffing is needed for Years 1–2** — that role and its associated cost are deferred to the future combat phase.

### Other paid/volunteer roles needed per event

- **Event director / logistics lead** (likely a founder role in Year 1)
- **On-site first aid/medical** — a reasonable, lower-cost precaution for general outdoor-event risk (heat exposure, minor injuries, allergic reactions) rather than the near-mandatory, higher-tier staffing that physical combat would require; confirm any county-specific EMS/first-aid staffing thresholds once a target county is chosen
- **Check-in / registration staff**
- **Quest/game design and "plot" staff** (writes the storylines, runs key NPC roles — likely founders + trusted community members in Year 1, per the stated founder asset of "people who have participated in similar 5-day LARP events"; theater-of-mind/non-combat systems place more narrative weight on this role since resolution happens through roleplay/mechanics rather than physical combat)
- **Photography/videography staff** (see §5.4)
- **F&B staff**, if food/drink is offered in-house rather than via a contracted vendor (see §5.5)

## 5.3 Ticket Tier Structure

Grounded directly in the pricing precedents from `01-Market-and-Comparables.md`:

| Tier | Comparable precedent | Suggested Year 1 approach |
|---|---|---|
| **NPC/Volunteer** | Dystopia Rising: $55 discounted "Max Casting" (6-hr shift); Knight Realms: free | Discounted ticket (not free, to still cover base sanitation/food-service cost) in exchange for a shift |
| **BYO camping, standard player** | Dystopia Rising: $75–$100/weekend; Knight Realms: $50/weekend | Land in the **$60–$100/weekend** range for a comparable multi-day fantasy/thematic event — consistent with the established market, not underpriced (which would undersell the product) or wildly overpriced (which would price out the existing hobbyist base identified in our market sizing) |
| **Day pass** | Dystopia Rising: $55–$65 | Useful for the "curious newcomer" segment identified in the broader experience-economy market (§2.5 of Market doc) |
| **Fully-furnished glamping tier** | Glamping industry: $100–$400+/night depending on tier (`01-Market-and-Comparables.md` §2.1E) | Price the "fully supplied, themed canvas tent" tier at **$150–$300/night equivalent** folded into a multi-day package — this is the highest-margin tier and the most direct product differentiator vs. every comparable LARP org, none of which offer this |
| **VIP/opt-out add-ons** | Dystopia Rising: $125–$250 for NPC-shift opt-out | Offer similar "opt out of volunteer shift" upsell |

**Honest note:** none of our comparables charge anywhere near enough per ticket to cover land acquisition debt service from ticket revenue alone at low attendance — this reinforces the finding in `01-Market-and-Comparables.md` that the **rental/hosting revenue stream and the premium glamping tier are more important to near-term viability than base ticket price**, since base tickets are constrained by what an established market already expects to pay.

## 5.4 Phone-Free / Photography & Videography Service Design

This is a genuine product differentiator tied directly to the mission statement (`00-Overview-and-Outline.md` §1.2) — no comparable LARP org in our research offers this as a formal in-house service; it is closer to what wedding/event photographers or theme-park "PhotoPass"-style services do.

**Recommended structure:**
- **Phone check-in (optional, not mandatory in Year 1):** offering — not requiring — guests the ability to check phones at a staffed station reduces liability around lost/damaged devices and legal complexity of a mandatory confiscation policy; mandatory phone bans raise real legal/safety concerns (a guest needs an emergency contact method) that a voluntary system avoids.
- **In-house photo/video staff or contracted freelancers** roam the event capturing in-character moments, similar to a wedding photographer package model.
- **Monetization options** (to test in Year 1): (a) a flat add-on fee bundled into the premium ticket tier, (b) an à la carte digital photo package sold post-event (common wedding/event-photography monetization model), or (c) bundling it into the "fully-furnished" tier only as a differentiator, keeping it complimentary at that tier and paid separately for base-tier guests.
- **This is a genuine paid role in Year 1**, not a founder side task — image/video quality directly supports marketing for future events (a legitimate dual-purpose cost: guest service **and** the Company's own marketing content pipeline).

## 5.5 Food & Beverage Operations

Real cost data for planning:

| Service style | Cost per person | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Drop-off buffet catering | $18–$35/person | Lowest complexity, no service staff |
| Full buffet catering (staffed) | $25–$75/person | Matches "optional meal plan" positioning |
| Plated/full-service | $45–$120/person | Not appropriate for Year 1 scale/budget |

**Recommendation:** offer food as an **optional add-on**, not a bundled/mandatory cost, consistent with the "bring your own gear or pay for convenience" tiering philosophy already established for lodging. A **drop-off or self-serve buffet model ($18–$35/person)** is the realistic Year 1 starting point; in-character "tavern" or "feast hall" theming can be layered onto a relatively simple catering operation without needing full-service staffing costs. **Alcohol service is a separate, deferred decision** per `03-Regulatory-Legal-and-Insurance.md` §4.2 (licensing + well-water testing delay).

## 5.7 Key Takeaways Feeding Later Sections

1. **Quarterly cadence (3–4 events/year) for Year 1 is realistic** and matches founder targets — monthly cadence (Knight Realms-style) is a mature-stage goal, not a Year 1 target.
2. **Start with Halfling Hollow for narrative/execution reasons, not safety ranking** — with combat deferred to Phase 3+, all Year 1–2 themes (including Wastelands) share a comparable non-combat risk profile; theme order is now a creative/tone decision the founders can adjust freely.
3. **NPC/volunteer-shift-for-discount is the correct staffing cost control**, directly modeled on Dystopia Rising's proven mechanism.
4. **A lighter staffing floor (1–2 general safety/stewarding staff even at 30–50 attendees) applies under the non-combat model** — the weapons-check station role and the combat-driven "2+ dedicated responder" floor from the earlier draft are deferred along with combat itself.
5. **Ticket pricing should track the established market ($60–$100/weekend base)**, not be set arbitrarily — the premium glamping tier ($150–$300/night equivalent) is the more important lever for margin, not the base ticket.
6. **Photo/video service is a genuine paid Year 1 role with dual value** (guest product + marketing asset pipeline) — budget it as real labor, not a founder afterthought.
7. **Per-event operating costs (power, tents, F&B, portable sanitation) are now reasonably quantified** and ready to feed into the recurring cost model in `08-Financial-Projections.md`.
