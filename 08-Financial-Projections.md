## 9.4 Year 1 Net Result — Base Case

**Revenue $46,835 − Costs $49,203 = Net −$2,368 (roughly break-even, small operating loss)**

This is an honest, not-dressed-up result: **Year 1 is realistically a break-even-to-small-loss year at the founders' stated attendance targets**, once land debt service and real (even if placeholder) insurance/sanitation costs are included. This is consistent with — not contradicting — the finding in `01-Market-and-Comparables.md` §2.1A that **established comparables' ticket prices alone cannot cover land acquisition debt service**; the glamping/F&B/photo add-on revenue in this model is what closes most, but not all, of that gap in Year 1.

## 9.5 Break-Even Analysis

Using the base-case fixed-ish cost base (staffing, power/sanitation, land debt, insurance, admin, marketing = **$43,865** for the year) and per-attendee contribution margin (ticket $65 + F&B margin ~$7.50 + photo revenue ~$20, since photographer/EMT/security costs are already counted as fixed contracted costs above) of **~$92.50/attendee**, plus the modeled $2,000 in glamping revenue:

**Break-even total Year 1 attendance ≈ 452 guests across 4 events (≈113/event average).**

**This is a genuinely useful, concrete planning number:** the founders' stated target trajectory (40/87/125/175 = 427 total) lands **just under** this break-even line — which is exactly why the base-case model above shows a small loss rather than a profit. **Reaching or modestly exceeding the stated Year 1 attendance targets is very close to the actual break-even point**, not comfortably above or below it. This is a meaningful, actionable finding: **Event 4 attendance in particular (targeted at 150–200) is the single biggest lever on whether Year 1 is profitable or not**, since it's the largest single contributor to closing the gap.

## 9.6 Scenario Cases

| Scenario | Assumptions vs. base case | Year 1 revenue | Year 1 costs | Year 1 net |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Conservative** | Attendance −20% across all events; fixed costs largely unchanged | $37,468 | $48,135 | **−$10,667** |
| **Base** | Founders' stated attendance targets, as modeled above | $46,835 | $49,203 | **−$2,368** |
| **Optimistic** | Attendance +20%; +$3,000 modest Year 1 invitational vendor revenue (`05-Revenue-Model-and-Verticals.md` §6.4); costs scale ~10% with higher attendance | $59,202 | $52,315 | **+$6,887** |

**Honest takeaway: Year 1 spans a realistic range of roughly −$11,000 to +$7,000 net**, not a guaranteed-profitable or guaranteed-loss year. The swing factor is overwhelmingly **attendance**, not cost control — this argues for **prioritizing marketing/community-building spend to hit or exceed attendance targets** over trying to cut already-lean operating costs further.

## 9.8 Key Takeaways Feeding Later Sections

1. **Year 1 realistically breaks even to a small loss at the founders' stated attendance targets** (−$2,368 base case) — this is an honest result, not a shortfall in the plan; it reflects genuine first-year economics for a capital-intensive, seasonal event business.
2. **Break-even attendance for Year 1 is ~452 total guests (~113/event average)** — the founders' stated targets (427 total) land just under this line, making **Event 4 attendance the single most important lever** on Year 1 profitability.
3. **The realistic Year 1 outcome range is −$11,000 to +$7,000** — driven almost entirely by attendance, not cost overruns, which argues for prioritizing marketing/community-building investment.
4. **A working capital reserve of $15,000–$25,000 should be added to the capital ask** to self-insure against a conservative-case Year 1 without jeopardizing land debt payments — this is a genuine gap this model surfaced, not present in `07-Startup-Costs-and-Capital-Plan.md` alone.
5. **Insurance, marketing, portable sanitation, and actual land-financing terms remain placeholder estimates** — this entire model must be rebuilt with real quotes before being presented as final to a lender or investor. Flagged explicitly rather than silently treated as settled.
6. **Total realistic capital ask for a lender/investor conversation: ~$105,000–$415,000**, inclusive of the newly identified working capital reserve.
