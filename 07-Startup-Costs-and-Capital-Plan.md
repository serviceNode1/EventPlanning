## 8.1 Phase 0 — Formation and Pre-Acquisition (Lowest-Cost Phase, Do This First)

Real, sourced entity-formation costs:

| Item | Kentucky | Tennessee |
|---|---|---|
| Articles of Organization filing fee | $40 | $300 |
| Annual report | $15/year | $300/year |
| Minimum annual entity tax | $175/year (Kentucky LLET, applies regardless of revenue) | None (Tennessee has no LLET-equivalent, but the $300/year annual report is effectively the recurring cost) |
| County clerk recording fee | ~$13–$20 | Varies by county |

**Kentucky is dramatically cheaper to form and maintain an LLC in ($40 filing / ~$190/year ongoing) vs. Tennessee ($300 filing / $300/year ongoing)** — this is a real, quantifiable factor if the founders have flexibility on which state to domicile the Company in, independent of where the land itself ends up (the LLC's home state and the land's location do not have to be the same state, though operating in a state where the LLC isn't domiciled typically requires a "foreign qualification" filing in that state too — a real added cost/step if the land and the LLC's home state differ).

**Other Phase 0 costs (order-of-magnitude, not all fully sourced — flagged where estimated):**

| Item | Estimated cost | Source basis |
|---|---|---|
| Attorney-drafted multi-member operating agreement (manager-managed, with vesting/repurchase provisions per `06-Org-Structure-and-Governance.md`) | **Not directly sourced — get a real quote.** A DIY/template agreement costs $0–$500, but given the vesting, supermajority-voting, and profits-interest complexity flagged in §7.3 of that document, a **real attorney-drafted agreement is strongly recommended over a template** for this specific structure | Flagged as an action item, not invented |
| CUP application fee (county-dependent) | $300–$2,000 | `03-Regulatory-Legal-and-Insurance.md` §4.2 |
| Registered agent service (if not self-serving as agent) | $0–$300/year | Legal formation market data |
| EIN (federal tax ID) | Free | IRS |

**Phase 0 total: roughly $650–$3,000**, depending on legal counsel choice and state — genuinely the cheapest phase, and the one that should be completed before any capital-intensive land search begins, since the LLC needs to exist to hold title/enter contracts.

## 8.2 Phase 1 — Land Acquisition

Directly from `01-Market-and-Comparables.md` §2.2 and `02-Site-and-Real-Estate.md` §3.1 (target: 30–60 acres):

| Land type/region | Price/acre | Cost for 30 acres | Cost for 60 acres |
|---|---|---|---|
| Eastern Kentucky pasture/woodland (lowest-cost band) | $1,600–$2,400 | $48,000–$72,000 | $96,000–$144,000 |
| Central Kentucky woodland | $4,100–$5,200 | $123,000–$156,000 | $246,000–$312,000 |
| Central Kentucky pasture | $5,600–$6,100 | $168,000–$183,000 | $336,000–$366,000 |
| Tennessee rural counties (Hancock, Perry, Wayne) | $5,000–$19,000 | $150,000–$570,000 | $300,000–$1,140,000 |

**Realistic planning band: $50,000–$250,000** for a 30–60 acre parcel, assuming the founders target the lower-cost rural bands identified in `01-Market-and-Comparables.md` (eastern KY or low-cost TN rural counties) rather than the Tennessee exurban/high band, which should be avoided per that document's explicit guidance.

**Financing structure:** per `02-Site-and-Real-Estate.md` §3.4, **lease-to-own or seller financing is the recommended path**, not a cash purchase — this materially changes the *initial* cash outlay from the full purchase price above to a **down payment (commonly 10–20% in seller-financed rural land deals) plus ongoing debt service**, which belongs in `08-Financial-Projections.md`'s cash flow model rather than as a single Phase 1 capital outlay. **This document presents the full acquisition cost as the capital the Company needs access to (via financing), not necessarily cash it needs on hand.**

## 8.3 Phase 2 — Site Development (Real Cost Data)

Site development costs scale with the developed footprint, not the full acreage — a 30–60 acre parcel doesn't need every acre graded/cleared, only the portion used for parking, camping pads, driveways, and communal structures (estimated at **10–15 developed acres** at our Year 1–2 scale, per the site-density benchmarks in `02-Site-and-Real-Estate.md` §3.1).

| Item | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Site clearing & grading (10–15 developed acres) | $1,500–$8,000/acre → **$15,000–$120,000** | Wide range depends heavily on vegetation density/terrain — a wooded parcel costs meaningfully more to clear than open pasture, a real factor in site selection beyond just price/acre |
| Entrance road/interior driveway network | $5,000–$40,000/acre-equivalent → realistic total **$15,000–$50,000** for a modest gravel entrance + interior access road (not a full paved road network) | Gravel is standard and appropriate for this use case — a paved road is not necessary at Year 1–2 scale |
| Well drilling | $3,000–$15,300 (avg ~$5,500) | `02-Site-and-Real-Estate.md` §3.3 |
| Residential-grade septic (staff/admin facilities only) | $3,500–$12,500 | `02-Site-and-Real-Estate.md` §3.3 — commercial-grade septic ($15,000–$80,000) is explicitly deferred until attendance regularly exceeds ~150 guests, per that document |
| Soil/perc testing (must happen **before** purchase, not after) | $300–$1,200 | Confirms septic feasibility — a hard gate, not optional |
| Permits/impact fees (site development side, separate from the CUP itself) | $1,000–$5,000 (typical), up to $25,000 in complex cases | General land-development cost data — actual figure is county-specific |

**Phase 2 total: realistically $35,000–$215,000**, with the wide range driven almost entirely by (a) how wooded vs. open the chosen parcel is, and (b) how long the entrance/interior road network needs to be. **This is the single best argument for prioritizing open-pasture or already-partially-cleared parcels during site selection** (`02-Site-and-Real-Estate.md` §3.2) — it can cut this phase's cost by more than half.

## 8.4 Phase 3 — Event-Ready Equipment and Launch Capex

| Item | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Glamping units (fully-furnished canvas tents, premium ticket tier) | $15,000–$40,000/unit | `01-Market-and-Comparables.md` §2.1E. **Recommendation: start with 2–3 units in Year 1** ($30,000–$120,000), not a full glamping "village" — this tier can scale as demand is proven, rather than over-investing before the first event validates guest willingness to pay the $150–$300/night premium price point (`05-Revenue-Model-and-Verticals.md` §6.2) |
| Generators, large communal tent, portable sanitation | **Rent, don't buy, in Year 1–2** | `04-Operations-and-Event-Design.md` §5.6 already prices these as **per-event rental costs** ($55–$380/day for generators, $2,000–$3,000/multi-day rental for a large communal tent) — purchasing this equipment outright is a real future option once event frequency increases enough to make ownership cheaper than repeated rental, but is **not a Year 1 capital requirement** and should stay off this capex list for now |
| First-year insurance premium | **Not sourced — real quote required** | `03-Regulatory-Legal-and-Insurance.md` §4.5/§4.6 — action item before this line can be filled in with a real number |
| Basic signage, way-finding, initial branding/website | **Not sourced — get real vendor quotes** | Flagged as a genuine cost category that exists but isn't fabricated with an invented number here |
| Weapons-check infrastructure / combat-safety equipment | **$0 — deferred to Phase 3+ combat introduction** | Per `03-Regulatory-Legal-and-Insurance.md` §4.3, non-active for Years 1–2 |

**Phase 3 total (excluding the unsourced insurance/marketing lines): roughly $30,000–$120,000**, driven almost entirely by how many glamping units the Company commits to before the first event.

## 8.5 Consolidated Startup Capital Range

| Phase | Low estimate | High estimate |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 0 — Formation | $650 | $3,000 |
| Phase 1 — Land acquisition (full price; actual cash need is lower under lease-to-own, §8.2) | $50,000 | $250,000 |
| Phase 2 — Site development | $35,000 | $215,000 |
| Phase 3 — Event-ready equipment | $30,000 | $120,000 |
| **Total (full-price land assumption)** | **~$115,650** | **~$588,000** |

**Two honest caveats on this range:**
1. **This is not a "cash needed on day one" number** — per §8.2, land acquisition should be financed (lease-to-own/seller financing), which converts most of the Phase 1 figure into a down payment plus ongoing debt service rather than upfront cash. The realistic **upfront cash need is closer to Phase 0 + Phase 2 + Phase 3 + a land down payment (10–20% of Phase 1)**, i.e., roughly **$70,000–$390,000** depending heavily on parcel choice (wooded vs. cleared, acreage, region) and how many glamping units are purchased before Event 1.
2. **Insurance and marketing/branding costs are real but not sourced with specific numbers** — these should not be silently treated as $0 in `08-Financial-Projections.md`; they need real quotes before that document is finalized.

## 8.6 Phased Capital Draw Structure (Bank/Investor-Friendly Framing)

Consistent with the milestone-based capital draw approach appropriate for lender/investor presentations (per `00-Overview-and-Outline.md`'s stated goal of a bank/investor-ready plan):

| Draw | Tied to milestone | Approximate capital |
|---|---|---|
| Draw 1 | LLC formation + land under contract/lease-to-own agreement signed | $650–$3,000 + land down payment (10–20% of Phase 1) |
| Draw 2 | Perc test passed, site development begins | $35,000–$215,000 |
| Draw 3 | Site development complete, Event 1 preparation | $30,000–$120,000 |

**Structuring the capital ask this way — tied to concrete, verifiable milestones rather than a single lump sum — is standard practice for both bank lending and investor/crowdfunding presentations**, and directly supports the debt/revenue-share crowdfunding instrument recommendation already flagged in `03-Regulatory-Legal-and-Insurance.md` §4.1.

## 8.7 Key Takeaways Feeding Later Sections

1. **Total startup capital realistically ranges from ~$115,000 to ~$590,000** depending heavily on land cost region, terrain (wooded vs. cleared), and how many glamping units are purchased before Event 1 — this is the honest range, not a single invented number.
2. **The realistic upfront cash need (~$70,000–$390,000) is meaningfully lower than the full capital range**, because land acquisition should be financed (lease-to-own/seller financing) rather than paid in cash — this is the single biggest lever the founders have to reduce Day 1 cash requirements.
3. **Kentucky is a materially cheaper state to domicile the LLC in** ($40 filing / ~$190/year) vs. Tennessee ($300 filing / $300/year) — a real, quantifiable factor if the founders have flexibility on entity domicile.
4. **Site development cost is highly sensitive to parcel terrain** — prioritizing open/pasture land over heavily wooded land during site selection can cut Phase 2 costs by more than half.
5. **Glamping unit count is the biggest controllable Phase 3 lever** — starting with 2–3 units instead of a full "village" defers capital until the premium tier's demand is actually proven at Event 1–2.
6. **Insurance premiums and marketing/branding costs remain unsourced and must be quoted directly** before `08-Financial-Projections.md` can be finalized — flagged here rather than filled in with invented numbers.
7. **A milestone-based, three-draw capital structure** (formation/land → site development → equipment/launch) is the right framing for a bank or investor presentation.
